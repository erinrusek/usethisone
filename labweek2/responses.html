Response on Found Are.na Site:
http://berlin.rietveldacademie.nl/wendy36/index.html




Stumbling onto this website titled Wendy 36, it became immediately clear that
what I was looking at was an archival system. Dragging the cursor across the
screen is like shuffling through library books on a shelf, or flipping through a
journal. Once I looked closer into the text, the story was revealed to me in
snippets of text conversations, candid photographs and design work. Music was
also included, but because the site is four years old, the soundcloud links
weren’t functioning. The larger narrative revolves around the lives of a group
of third-year graphic design students, and archives a six week period of
their academic careers.

The website although graphically tidy, emitted a strong sense of nostalgia for me
(even though I have never been a student at the Gerrit Rietveld Academie
Amsterdam). I believe this sentiment originates from the amount of intimate
messages between students as well as humorous depictions of their daily life
scattered here and there.

I picked this site after brainstorming personal website ideas from last Friday’s
class. I realized I really wanted to make my own site an archive of my work as
well as a sort of time capsule. The conceptual difference between Wendy36 and
the site I’m aiming to create I believe, is the idea of separate “folders” of
time periods. Unlike Wendy36, my website will not be isolated down to a
specific period of time.
